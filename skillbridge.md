SkillBridge is a mobile micro-task platform that connects
anyone with a problem to someone nearby who can solve it — fast. Whether you
are a small business owner who needs a logo touched up, a working adult who
needs a spreadsheet fixed, a fresh graduate looking to build a portfolio, or
simply someone who needs a task done today, SkillBridge makes it easy to post a
request and get matched with a skilled individual within minutes. The platform
supports flexible arrangements including paid gigs, skill exchanges,
reward-based tasks, or even free community help — making it accessible
regardless of budget or background.

SkillBridge is designed for a broad public audience across
multiple user groups. Working adults who encounter small but time-sensitive
skill gaps in their daily or professional lives — such as needing a document
translated, a spreadsheet fixed, or a presentation polished. Freelancers and
fresh graduates who want to earn income, build their portfolio, and gain
real-world experience by offering their skills on a flexible basis. Small
business owners who need quick, affordable access to skilled individuals without
the overhead of hiring full-time staff or expensive agencies. Job seekers who
can both offer skills to earn while they search, and access peer support to
improve their applications, CVs, or interview readiness. In short, SkillBridge
serves anyone who has a skill to offer or a task that needs to be done —
regardless of background, income, or education level.

The global demand for flexible, skill-based work is growing
at an unprecedented rate. Gig work now accounts for up to 12% of the global
labor force, according to the World Bank. Upwork The gig economy had a market
size of $556.7 billion in 2024, and is expected to more than triple to $1,847
billion by 2032. World Economic Forum This signals a massive, underserved
population of workers and task-seekers who need a faster, more accessible way
to connect.

At the same time, the skills gap is a growing crisis for
individuals and businesses alike. 87% of companies worldwide are already aware
that they either have a skills gap or will have one within a few years,
according to McKinsey & Company. Instride 70% of executives say their
businesses are suffering financially because their workforces lack the right
competencies. ADVISOR Magazine Yet traditional solutions — hiring agencies,
formal training programs, or tutoring services — are too slow and too expensive
for everyday, small-scale needs.

There is also a clear human behavior shift underway. Over
150 million workers in North America and Western Europe have already left
traditional employment to join the gig economy. TeamStage In countries like
India and Mexico, 97% of people are open to freelance, contract, or independent
contractor work. Fortunly This shows that people globally are ready and willing
to exchange skills flexibly — they just lack the right platform to do it
efficiently. SkillBridge directly addresses this gap by providing a fast,
inclusive, and flexible matching system that connects real needs with real
skills, in real time.
